Option 1 — Dragonfly + AI Layer

Maximize what Hilberts already owns. Restart Dragonfly, layer AI automation on top for bid intake and proposal QA. No new platforms introduced.

Foundation path
Activates AlphaTakeoff for blueprint AI, automates the morning bid queue, and adds a Claude review gate on every ABK proposal — all within Dragonfly and the PhaseAlpha ecosystem.
4–6 weeks · ~90 hrs
Implementation phases
1A
Dragonfly foundation restart
Rebuild pipeline with statuses: Invited → Reviewed → Quoted → Follow-Up Needed → Won/Lost. Import contacts, configure custom fields, train Tom, Belinda, and Dave.
Weeks 1–4
1B
AI bid intake automation
Outlook → Make → Dragonfly. Claude monitors ITB emails, extracts project name / bid date / GC contact / drawing links, creates opportunity records, and flags drawings for Tom's morning review queue.
Weeks 3–6
1C
AlphaTakeoff activation
Upload bid drawings — AI counts GRDs, VAVs, FCUs, dampers, louvers in minutes. Takeoff sheets auto-attach to Dragonfly projects. Post-award room schedules generated automatically, segmented by floor.
Weeks 5–8
1D
Proposal QA gate
Claude reviews ABK output before it leaves the office — flags scope gaps and pricing anomalies. Human approves; Claude drafts the outbound email and sends via Outlook connector.
Weeks 6–10
Pain points addressed
Bid intake

Blueprint takeoff
4–6 hrs manual → minutes via AlphaTakeoff
Proposal QA
Claude review gate before every send
Room schedules

Strengths
Builds on existing Dragonfly investment — no new platform cost

Option 2 — Dragonfly as Full CRM

Use Dragonfly's custom fields, pipeline statuses, and API layer as a true CRM — structured follow-up automation, win/loss analytics, and contact records — all without adding a second platform.

Recommended starting point
Everything in Option 1, plus Dragonfly configured as a real CRM with Make-triggered follow-up sequences and a weekly analytics digest. Single platform, direct PhaseAlpha relationship — Tom already knows Joe Lewis.
6–9 weeks · ~110 hrs
"Could it be more of that [a CRM]? Maybe. We have to talk to Joe on that one." — Tom Hilberts, Feb 26 2026
What this adds over Option 1
+A
Dragonfly custom record types as CRM backbone
Work with Joe Lewis to build Contacts, Customers, and Opportunities as first-class Dragonfly records. Status fields on opportunities drive all downstream automation triggers. One platform; everything searchable.
Scope confirmed pending PhaseAlpha call
+B
Structured follow-up via Make automation
When quote status changes to "Sent," Make fires a Day 3 and Day 10 follow-up email (Claude-drafted, human-approved template). High-value jobs above a set threshold trigger a direct alert to Dave or Tom to call personally.
Weeks 6–9
+C
Weekly analytics digest
Claude + Make pull Dragonfly data every Friday: close rate by customer, open quotes by rep, high-value jobs needing attention. Tom gets a clean summary email without manually touching a spreadsheet.
Weeks 9–12
Pain points addressed
Bid intake

Blueprint takeoff
AlphaTakeoff native — same PhaseAlpha ecosystem
Proposal QA
Claude gate + auto-send on approval
Quote follow-up
Day 3/10 triggers via Make — no manual tracking
Analytics / dashboard
Weekly Claude digest — close rate, open quotes, high-value alerts

Option 3 — Dragonfly + GoHighLevel

Add GoHighLevel as a dedicated marketing automation layer once Dragonfly is stable. Dragonfly runs operations; GHL runs outbound campaigns and full CRM pipeline visibility.

Advanced — 90-day expansion
Recommended as a Phase 2 expansion after Option 2 is stable. Unlocks hospital OR campaigns, destratification fan outreach, and full close-rate dashboards through GHL.
8–12 weeks · ~140 hrs
How the two platforms divide responsibility
Dragonfly handles
Project files, drawings, POs, order history Room schedules and AlphaTakeoff outputs Internal work tasks and floor releases Bid status pipeline and opportunity records
GoHighLevel handles
Customer contact records and deal pipeline Day 3/10 follow-up email and SMS sequences Outbound marketing campaigns Close rate dashboards and rep performance
Marketing automation unlocked by GHL
M1
Hospital OR systems campaign
Claude builds a target list of facility managers at Eastern PA hospitals. Monthly outreach sequence in GHL — going direct instead of exclusively through engineers and architects.
M2
Destratification fan campaign
Target facilities managers in Eastern PA using the PPL energy rebate angle Tom identified. Claude drafts copy; GHL sequences the outreach; website and LinkedIn tied in.
M3
Quote follow-up sequences (GHL native)
Day 3: "Hi Bob, following up on the quote for [Project]." Day 10: second touch. High-value threshold triggers a personal call from Dave or Tom.
Pros & considerations
Strengths
Solves all eight pain points including marketing
Hospital OR and fan campaigns become executable
GHL dashboard gives Tom real close-rate visibility
Full Claude + Make + GHL automation loop
Considerations

GHL ~$97–$297/month additional recurring cost

Option 4 — GoHighLevel First

A contingency path only. If the PhaseAlpha evaluation reveals Dragonfly's API cannot support the integrations needed, GHL becomes the single platform for CRM, operations, and marketing.

Contingency — not the recommended starting point
Only relevant if the Joe Lewis call surfaces a hard technical or pricing blocker with Dragonfly. Tom's existing PhaseAlpha relationship and Dragonfly's HVAC-native design make this unlikely to be necessary.
Contingency · ~130 hrs
